## Summary:

The 10th Socratic Seminar of BitDevs Gitega was dedicated to the official presentation and deep exploration of the upcoming 8-month accelerated incubation program supported by the Human Rights Foundation (HRF). This program aims to support and guide Burundian developers in building the first Bitcoin-related Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) solutions made in Burundi.

The session marked a strategic shift from purely technical exploration to structured ecosystem building. Participants were introduced to the full architecture of the incubation program, including its objectives, timeline, selection criteria, and expected outputs. The goal is to empower a cohort of local developers to move from learning to building, contributing to Bitcoin through practical, open-source solutions adapted to the Burundian context.


During the seminar, participants explored:
 · The vision and rationale behind the HRF-supported incubation program

· The structure of the 8-month program (monthly BitDevs, mentorship, hackathon, and continuous building phases)

· The role of FOSS in strengthening Bitcoin adoption and developer sovereignty

· Expectations for participating developers, including active contribution and project delivery

· The types of solutions targeted (wallet tools, local infrastructure, payment tools, developer utilities, etc.)

· Incentive mechanisms, including Proof of Work rewards in sats


The discussion generated strong enthusiasm among participants, with many expressing interest in joining the program and contributing to locally relevant Bitcoin solutions. The session also helped clarify the long-term vision of positioning Gitega as a hub for Bitcoin development in Burundi.

The seminar maintained its Socratic format, encouraging open discussion, critical thinking, and collaborative ideation around how to successfully execute the program and ensure meaningful outputs.

★	Developers now have a long-term framework (8 months) to collaborate, build, and deliver real projects.
